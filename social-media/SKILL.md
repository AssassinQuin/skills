# Social Media

Social posts are short-form content written for a specific platform and published directly without human review. Every word matters, and platform conventions differ enough that one-size-fits-all writing fails.

## Core Constraints

These rules apply to every social post regardless of platform:

1. **No external links.** Most social platforms penalize posts containing links by reducing their reach. Never include URLs in the post body (Reddit link posts are the exception — see `references/platforms.md`).
2. **No placeholders.** Posts are published directly. Any placeholder (`[Link: ...]`, `[Image: ...]`, `[Brand Name]`) appears verbatim to followers.
3. **No emdashes.** Emdashes signal AI-generated content to readers. Use commas or separate sentences.
4. **No image placeholders.** Never include `![Visual: ...]`, `![Image: ...]`, or `[Image description: ...]`.
5. **Brand name appears naturally.** Mention the brand in context, not as a forced insertion.
6. **Current year for time-sensitive content.** Use the current year for trends, stats, and recommendations.

## Platform Quick Reference

| Platform    | Character limit | Target length    | Tone                          |
| ----------- | --------------- | ---------------- | ----------------------------- |
| LinkedIn    | 3,000           | 300-500 chars    | Professional, informative     |
| X (Twitter) | 280             | 280 chars max    | Punchy, direct                |
| Threads     | 500             | 300-500 chars    | Conversational, authentic     |
| Instagram   | 2,200           | 200-300 chars    | Story-driven, visual-first    |
| Facebook    | 63,000          | 400-600 chars    | Conversational, personal      |
| Reddit      | 40,000          | 500-1,000+ chars | Community-first, value-driven |

## Writing Approach

### Hook First

The first line determines whether someone reads the rest. On most platforms, only the first 1-2 lines display before "See more." Front-load the most compelling idea.

Good hooks:

- Open with an insight or counterintuitive claim
- Pose a specific question the post will answer
- State a concrete result or statistic

Bad hooks:

- "In today's digital landscape..."
- "We're excited to announce..."
- "Did you know that..." (overused)

### Tone Calibration

Match the platform's conversational norms:

- **LinkedIn**: Authoritative and informative. Lead with insight. Support with evidence. End with a question or recommendation.
- **X**: Sharp and direct. One idea per post. No filler.
- **Threads**: Casual and authentic. Feels like talking to a friend who happens to be knowledgeable.
- **Instagram**: Story-driven. Emojis to break up text. Aspirational or instructional.
- **Facebook**: Personal and relatable. Longer form. Anecdotes work well.
- **Reddit**: Community-first. No overt promotion. Provide genuine value. Match the subreddit's norms.

### Character Budget Discipline

Stay within the target range, not just the hard limit. A LinkedIn post that fills all 3,000 characters reads like a blog post crammed into a feed. A tweet that hits exactly 280 characters leaves no room for a quote-retweet comment.

## Hashtags

| Platform  | Hashtag count | Notes                   |
| --------- | ------------- | ----------------------- |
| LinkedIn  | 3-5           | Relevant industry terms |
| X         | 2-3           | Trending or niche terms |
| Threads   | 0-2           | Optional, keep minimal  |
| Instagram | 10-15         | Mix of broad and niche  |
| Facebook  | 1-3           | Minimal, optional       |
| Reddit    | 0             | Not used on Reddit      |

Place hashtags at the end of the post, not inline. Inline hashtags break reading flow.

## Call to Action

Every post needs a purpose, but not every post needs a hard CTA. Match the CTA strength to the platform:

- **LinkedIn**: Ask a question to drive comments, or recommend a specific action
- **X**: Implicit CTAs work ("What's your take?")
- **Threads**: Encourage replies, keep it casual
- **Instagram**: CTA in the last line ("Link in bio", "Save this for later")
- **Facebook**: Mid-post or end CTA
- **Reddit**: No CTA. Value-driven content earns engagement organically.

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing a social post, verify:

1. Character count is within the platform's target range
2. No external links, image placeholders, or bracketed instructions
3. Brand name appears naturally (not forced)
4. Hook is in the first 1-2 lines
5. Hashtags follow platform conventions (count and placement)
6. Tone matches the platform
7. No emdashes
