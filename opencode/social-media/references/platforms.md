# Platform-by-Platform Guide

Detailed writing rules, structural patterns, and engagement tactics for each supported social platform.

## LinkedIn

### Character Limits

- Hard limit: 3,000 characters
- Target: 300-500 characters
- "See more" truncation: ~140 characters on mobile, ~210 on desktop

### Structure

The typical LinkedIn post follows one of these patterns:

**Insight post (most common):**

1. Opening insight or observation (1-2 sentences)
2. Supporting context or data (2-3 short paragraphs)
3. Key takeaway or recommendation
4. Question to drive comments

**Story post:**

1. Personal anecdote or observation
2. What happened and why it mattered
3. Lesson or insight
4. Connection to broader theme

**List post:**

1. Problem or question framing (1 sentence)
2. 3-5 numbered or bulleted points
3. Closing thought or question

### Tone

- Professional but not stiff
- Authoritative without being preachy
- Informative over promotional
- Avoid buzzwords: "synergy", "thought leadership", "paradigm shift"

### Hashtags

- 3-5 relevant industry hashtags
- Place at the end of the post, separated by a blank line
- Mix broad terms (#Marketing) with specific ones (#B2BSaaSMarketing)

### Common Mistakes

- Writing a full article instead of a post (stay under 500 chars)
- Leading with company promotion instead of audience value
- Using the company page voice when a personal voice works better
- Hashtag stuffing (more than 5 looks spammy)

## X (Twitter)

### Character Limits

- Hard limit: 280 characters
- Target: Use the full 280 when needed, but shorter often performs better
- Thread format available for longer topics

### Structure

**Single tweet:**

- One idea, one sentence, one CTA (implicit or explicit)
- Front-load the key point

**Thread (for complex topics):**

1. Hook tweet that stands alone (this is what gets shared)
2. 3-7 supporting tweets, each self-contained
3. Final tweet with summary or CTA

### Tone

- Sharp, direct, opinionated
- No hedging ("might", "perhaps", "it could be argued")
- Conversational but informed
- Hot takes work if backed by substance

### Hashtags

- 2-3 maximum
- Niche or trending terms only
- Never start a tweet with a hashtag (unless intentionally narrowing reach)

### Common Mistakes

- Wasting characters on filler ("Just wanted to share that...")
- Writing for LinkedIn tone on X (too formal)
- Thread tweets that don't stand alone (each should make a point even without context)
- Using more than 3 hashtags (looks automated)

## Threads

### Character Limits

- Hard limit: 500 characters
- Target: 300-500 characters

### Structure

- Casual, single-thought posts work best
- 1-3 sentences is the sweet spot
- Carousel threads supported but optional

### Tone

- Conversational and authentic
- Casual, friendly language
- Like talking to a knowledgeable friend
- Encourage replies and engagement over likes

### Hashtags

- 0-2, optional
- The platform is still developing hashtag culture
- When used, keep them minimal and relevant

### Common Mistakes

- Cross-posting X content without adapting tone
- Being too formal or corporate
- Over-hashtagging (this isn't Instagram)

## Instagram

### Character Limits

- Hard limit: 2,200 characters (caption)
- Target: 200-300 characters for feed posts
- "More" truncation: ~125 characters on feed

### Structure

**Feed post caption:**

1. Hook in first line (this shows before "More")
2. Story or context (2-3 short lines)
3. CTA on last line ("Save this for later", "Tag someone who needs this")
4. Hashtags in a separate comment or after line breaks

**Carousel post (text-based):**

- Each slide is a standalone visual with text
- Caption supplements the slides, doesn't repeat them

### Tone

- Visual-first mindset (describe or complement the visual)
- Story-driven or inspirational
- Emojis to break up text and add personality
- Warmer and more personal than LinkedIn

### Hashtags

- 10-15 hashtags
- Mix of broad (#Marketing, #SmallBusiness) and niche (#B2BSaaSGrowth)
- Place in a separate comment or after several line breaks to keep the caption clean

### Common Mistakes

- Writing a caption that makes no sense without the image (the caption should add value independently)
- No CTA (Instagram rewards engagement, so prompt it)
- Using only broad hashtags (niche hashtags have better engagement rates)
- Ignoring the "More" truncation (hook must work in 125 chars)

## Facebook

### Character Limits

- Hard limit: 63,000 characters
- Target: 400-600 characters
- "See more" truncation: ~477 characters on desktop, ~110 on mobile

### Structure

**Standard post:**

1. Personal or relatable opening
2. Story or context (2-4 short paragraphs)
3. Key insight or recommendation
4. CTA (question, share prompt, or action)

**Link share post (when links are allowed):**

1. Brief framing of why the link matters (1-2 sentences)
2. Link generates its own preview card

### Tone

- Conversational and story-driven
- Personal anecdotes resonate
- Less formal than LinkedIn, more substantive than Threads
- Community-oriented

### Hashtags

- 1-3, minimal
- Facebook's algorithm doesn't weight hashtags heavily
- Use them for categorization, not discovery

### Common Mistakes

- Writing too much (Facebook posts over 80 words see declining engagement)
- Pure promotional content without a story
- Ignoring mobile truncation (~110 chars) when crafting the opening
- Cross-posting LinkedIn content without adjusting tone

## Reddit

### Character Limits

- Hard limit: 40,000 characters
- Target: 500-1,000+ characters (varies by subreddit)
- Title limit: 300 characters

### Structure

**Text post:**

1. Clear, descriptive title (this is what gets clicked)
2. Context and background
3. Core content (analysis, guide, question)
4. Conclusion or discussion prompt

**Comment-style engagement:**

- Reply to existing threads with substantive contributions
- Add genuine value before mentioning any brand

### Tone

- Community-first, never promotional
- Authentic, informed, slightly casual
- Value-driven: teach, analyze, or share genuine experience
- Match the subreddit's norms (some are formal, others very casual)

### Hashtags

- Not used on Reddit
- Tagging is done via subreddit choice, not hashtags

### Subreddit Etiquette

- Read the subreddit's rules before posting
- Lurk to understand the community's norms
- Self-promotion is usually limited to 10% of activity
- Disclose brand affiliation when relevant

### Common Mistakes

- Overt self-promotion (gets downvoted and removed)
- Ignoring subreddit-specific rules
- Using marketing tone ("We're thrilled to...", "Check out our...")
- Posting without engaging in comments
- Cross-posting the same content to multiple subreddits without adaptation
